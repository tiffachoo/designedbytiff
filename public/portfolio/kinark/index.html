Dedicated to supporting children’s mental health initiatives in Ontario, we are advancing the social and emotional well-being of children and youth

Help and Hope

All children deserve the chance to reach their full potential. Unfortunately, for the one out of every five children and youth who struggles with mental health problems, this can be a challenge. The Kinark Foundation offers hope and help by supporting organizations that promote the mental health and well-being of children and youth. Our donors and supporters are Champions for Children's Mental Health™, making a difference in the lives of children and youth and their families. Please join us.

Mental health problems are real, can be very serious and they affect families from all walks of life. Without effective intervention, mental health problems in young people can get worse, increasing the likelihood of school failure, family breakdown, substance abuse, criminal activity, and suicide, the leading cause of non-accidental death among 10-19 year olds. More than 70% of adults with mental illness report that signs and symptoms occurred during childhood and adolescence. Mental illness and the issues associated with mental health problems represent a crisis affecting every community in this country, touching almost every family.

The Kinark Foundation supports organizations that offer proven solutions and innovative practices that provide hope and help to children, youth, and families struggling with mental health issues.

Providing Solutions

Services that address the mental health needs of children and youth and their families have been proven to work, leading to significant improvements in school performance, social development, behaviour and self-esteem.

Reducing Stigma

Creating awareness and understanding of mental health issues leads to empathy and reduces stigma, paving the way for more help for children and families who need it.

Offering Hope

The Kinark Foundation offers hope today by funding prevention, education, respite and treatment programs that strengthen community capacity to respond to the mental health needs of children and youth and their families.
